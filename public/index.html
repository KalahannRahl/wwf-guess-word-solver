WORD GUESS SOLVER
How To Use
What this app does: Helps you solve the daily Guess Word puzzle in Words With Friends by finding every possible answer based on the target score and bonus tile positions.
1Enter Your Target Score
Look at the top of your Guess Word puzzle. You'll see "Score X pts to earn." Type that number into the score box. The app automatically shows the base word value (score ÷ 3) beneath it.
2Lock Bonus Positions
Tap each position button (1–5) to mark it as TL ×3 or DL ×2. One tap = TL, two taps = DL, three taps = clear. Locking these positions is your most powerful filter — it cuts results from hundreds of words down to a handful instantly.
3Hit Solve
The app returns all valid WWF words that can hit your target score, grouped by letter value pattern. Results are sorted by most likely letters first based on the game's own priority system.
4Use Your Guesses to Filter
After each guess the game gives you information. Use it:
✓Letters confirmed in the word → tap them in Must Have
≠Letters confirmed NOT in the word → tap them in Must Not
■Letter confirmed at exact position → enter it in the Position Lock boxes
Hit Solve again — the list shrinks dramatically.
5Lock TL/DL on Guess 3
By Round 3 the board shows you exactly which position is TL ×3 and which is DL ×2. Use the position lock buttons to confirm these exactly. Combined with your letter filters you'll typically be down to 1–3 words at this point.
Scoring Explained
The game always scores the same way:
🎯Your word is placed on a Triple Word (TW) row
×3The highest value letter gets tripled
×2The second highest value letter gets doubled
•The other 3 letters score at face value
×3Then the entire word score is multiplied ×3
Target Score ÷ 3 = the base word value you need.

Tips from Experienced Players
⚡Always set bonus positions first — it's the single biggest filter
📈High scores (66+) almost always have a 4pt or 5pt letter on the triple bonus
💎Very high scores (90+) likely contain X, Q, J, or Z
🧠Use the smart starter suggestions — they're calculated from your specific candidate list, not a generic dictionary
🔑Confirm positions as soon as you know them — one locked letter cuts the list in half
